iBrowe Browser Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how iBrowe Software collects, uses, and protects information from users of the iBrowe browser.It reflects our commitment to privacy by design and ensures full compliance with global privacy regulations such as GDPR and CCPA.

Privacy Policy

1. Overview iBrowe is a browser designed to protect user privacy by default. It blocks tracking and unwanted ads, and this policy applies to the use of the iBrowe browser and related features such as iBrowe Shields and iBrowe Rewards. We aim to minimize personal data collection and rely on privacy-preserving mechanisms like on-device processing.

2. Data We Collect iBrowe collects limited information, categorized as follows : Information Provided by Users : iBrowe Rewards : If you opt in to iBrowe Rewards (to view ads and earn iBrowe Attention Tokens or "iBOW"), you may be asked to provide information such as an email address for linking a crypto wallet (e.g., Uphold or Gemini). iBrowe Account (if any) : Email or subscription details (e.g., for iBrowe Premium). Contacting iBrowe: Such as email content sent to our support team. Automatically Collected Information : Usage Data : Anonymous data on how the browser functions, such as feature usage and button clicks.Statistics : For example, the number of ads blocked by iBrowe Shields.Crash Reports : Only if you opt in, crash data is sent anonymously (no IP or personal info).iBrowe Rewards and Ads: Ad interaction data is processed on-device. Tipping data is anonymized.Data We Do Not Collect:iBrowe does not collect browsing history, IP addresses linked to search activity, or any personally identifiable data.iBrowe Shields blocks third-party trackers.

3. Purpose of Data Use : To enhance browser performance and features, facilitate the operation of iBrowe Rewards and iBrowe Ads (e.g., managing BAT rewards), and conduct anonymized data analysis to support product development. We also use data to respond to user requests, such as providing customer support. iBrowe limits data collection to what is strictly necessary and avoids sharing personally identifiable information.

4. Data Sharing iBrowe shares data only in limited and specific circumstances : Service Providers : We work with trusted partners (such as server hosts or cryptocurrency wallet providers) who are contractually obligated to adhere to strict privacy and data protection standards. Legal Requirements : We may disclose data when required to comply with a valid legal process, such as a court order or applicable law.iBrowe Rewards: Certain information necessary for processing rewards may be shared with authorized partners, such as Uphold. These disclosures are limited to what is essential and are governed by strict privacy agreements. iBrowe does not sell your personal information under any circumstances.

5. Data Retention : We retain personal data (e.g., email for iBrowe Rewards) only as long as needed to provide services. Anonymized data may be stored longer for analytics.Users may request deletion of their personal data at any time.
‍
6. User Rights : You may access, correct, delete, or transfer your data, and object to its processing under laws like GDPR and CCPA. EU users may contact their local authority. Reach us at privacy@ibrowe.com.

7. Security : iBrowe uses security measures such as encryption and access controls. Most data is processed locally to reduce the risk of exposure.

8. Privacy Features : iBrowe blocks ads and trackers, prevents third-party tracking, offers reward-based private ads, and supports anonymous browsing with Tor mode.

‍9. Privacy Settings : Users can customize settings such as disabling anonymous usage metrics, adjusting iBrowe Shields (Standard or Aggressive), and more via Settings > Privacy and Security.

10. Contact : For questions about this policy, contact us at privacy@ibrowe.com.

11. Policy Updates : iBrowe may update this policy from time to time and will notify users via our website or other channels.The latest version takes effect upon publication.

Contacting iBrowe About Privacy
If you have questions about privacy or wish to exercise your rights under data protection laws, you may contact our Data Protection Officer and Privacy Team at privacy@ibrowe.com.

Our Privacy Commitment
At iBrowe, our policy is not to collect personal data unless it is strictly necessary to provide our services or to comply with specific legal obligations. We do not buy or sell consumer personal information. If we process your personal data under laws such as the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or U.S. state privacy laws like the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) or the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), you have the following rights : (A list of rights would typically follow here such as the right to access, delete, correct, object, or transfer data, which you've already covered in your “User Rights” section above.) :

1. Right to Be Informed : You have the right to know what personal data we collect, how we use it, and your associated rights. This is outlined in our product privacy policies and context-specific notices.
2. Right of Access : If we process your personal data, you may request a copy of that data.
3. Right to Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten) : Under certain conditions, you may request that we delete your personal data. Please note that we maintain a separate global policy for iBrowe Search results and the right to be forgotten.
4. Right to Rectification : You may request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
5. Right to Restrict Processing : In certain situations, you may request that we suspend processing your data.
6. Right to Object : In specific cases, you may object to our processing of your data for example, in profiling or fully automated decision-making.Note: iBrowe does not buy or sell consumer personal data.
7. Right to Data Portability : Where applicable, you may request to receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, enabling reuse.Please note: This does not apply to anonymized data.
